Rich Brown Website Analysis

Rich Brown

Visit Website

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

There are large, bold typography presenting R and B at the left of the interface with the moving background video about the designer himself, which grabbed my attention immediately due to the size and motion.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Saw the large title, background video, and some texts on the interface.
  • Tried to tap every text box and title to see if they can link to more information.
  • When hovering the plus symbol on the bottom right, the contact information will appear.
  • The large B typography on the bottom left can link to the Q&A about designer.
  • Scrolling down to explore more content.
  • Realized that when I scrolled to the projects part, even my action is scrolling down but the contents are scrolling to the right.
  • When I scroll to each project the color changed and there is a motion effects on every project page.
  • Stopped at the first project and clicked in to see more detail.
  • Scrolled horizontally and see there are a lot of images and some description about this project.
  • Return to the main page and viewed other projects.
  • There is an archive list when scrolled to the end.
  • Hovered over the list and there will have a visual box appeared to present the look of that archive.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time exploring the different projects by clicking into each one to see the details. I scrolled through the images and description of the project and realized that all projects have their own background colors which makes the experience feel unique. The project also contains many pictures so that people can immediately understand the appearance of the work without having to read the text carefully.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action during this interaction was continuously scrolling through the website’s content while I frequently clicked or hovered over different typography elements and symbols to see if it can trigger more animations, transitions, and information. Because the site has rich visualization, which bring out my curiosity that want to test which elements were functional.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I think the intended primary goal of this experience is to present designer’s portfolio in a creative and impactful way that can capture people’s attention immediately. This website not only just presents the designer’s works, but also reveals the skills and ideas of the designer himself through the interface design.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The site utilizes large bold typography and special layout and animation titles with various color backgrounds and immersive visuals on the interface to attract people’s attention, also includes minimal but impactful project descriptions and information on each page so users can easily understand the ideas. The horizontal and vertical scrolling method that changes constantly is also one of the features. These reveal the designer’s visual creativity and professional branding.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

This website feels like is for short but repeated experience. Users will spend around 5 to 10 minutes on every visit, they can explore few projects and then revisit for others later, which spend around 3 to 4 times.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The clear title and visually oriented design with simple scrolled navigation let the users can do quick browsing and find the project they interested quickly, but it also allows people to revisit and explore different contents.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The website references the magazine layout through large and bold typography as headline and visual backgrounds with text blocks. The motion-driven visualization and studio photos make the website feels like a professional digital photography gallery.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The reference suggests that I should engage with the visual first and try to interact with those large typography and motion visualization, not just focus on the textual information.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The layout of the website makes me feel like browsing a premium magazine or an art exhibition, the gallery-style navigation and the motion visuals let me explore with my own pace just like going to a museum, which feel immersive and inspired during the experience.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The website doesn’t have a menu to let the users jump directly to the contents they want, requiring them to scroll through everything. In addition, there is no clear home navigation icon, so I initially had to scroll all the way back up or refresh the page to return. It was only later that I discovered the “R” typography in the top-left corner functions as a home button.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part for me is the immersive animated background combined with the dynamic titles that change as I scroll. It creates a sense of movement that makes me feel engaging. I also enjoy the unique scrolling behavior, although I scroll down with my mouse, the interface actually moves horizontally, this transition let the website feel modern and high quality, also can presents the designer’s creativity. These special browsing experience not just grab people’s attention, also like exploring a interactive gallery.
